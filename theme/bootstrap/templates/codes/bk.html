In Brief

The offset front axle is an axle version for on-road vehicles with lowered frame or vehicle level above the front axle. The front axle is offset upwards in the area of the axle stubs. The axle body is therefore far lower so that the ground clearance, frame height and overall height of the bodywork above the front axle are reduced by the offset amount.

The offset front axle is part of the series equipment for all on-road vehicles. A distinction must be made between the following variants:

  • Offset 120 mm / 5 mm: for axles with a load capacity of 7.5 t and 9.0 t
  • Offset 150 mm / 5 mm: for axle with a load capacity of 8.0 t

Benefits and Arguments

  • Low vehicle centre of gravity owing to lowering of the vehicle frame
  • Comfortable entry into the cab thanks to reduced step height up to the bottom step
